How arbitrage works
Buy an asset where it’s cheaper, sell where it’s more expensive. SpreadNet scans multiple DEXs and routes on Solana to surface those price gaps in real time, net of costs.
1 Ingest
  • Live quotes from DEXs and aggregators (Jupiter, Orca, Raydium)
  • Unified data model for pairs, prices, depth
2 Normalize
  • Clean/align symbols, fees, liquidity metadata
  • Prepare candidates for scoring
3 Score
  • Net profitability after slippage, fees, TTL decay
  • Rank by risk-adjusted spread
4 Emit
  • Lightweight events over SSE/WebSocket to the live feed
  • Immediate UI updates, minimal payloads

SPREADNET(1) User Commands SpreadNet Manual
NAME
spreadnet - Solana arbitrage monitoring and execution platform
SYNOPSIS
spreadnet [--connect-wallet] [--monitor] [--execute]
DESCRIPTION

SpreadNet is a zero-cost, high-performance arbitrage engine tailored to the Solana blockchain. It continuously monitors prices across decentralized exchanges (DEXs) and routing services to identify risk-adjusted profit opportunities. The system is designed for low-latency signal generation and transparent decision-making, with a clean terminal-inspired interface for clarity and focus.

While the current MVP focuses on live detection and visualization, the platform architecture already anticipates non-custodial wallet connection and automated execution with configurable risk policies. Every component is modular, allowing incremental improvements without rewrites.

  • scan stream price quotes and on-chain data across multiple DEXs and aggregators
  • score compute net profitability after slippage, fees, and TTL decay
  • alert push minimal, real-time updates via WebSocket to the UI
  • execute forthcoming automated flow with wallet authorization and guardrails
ARCHITECTURE

The platform follows a hybrid design: a performance-oriented core for data ingestion and opportunity scoring, and a Python/Flask layer for real-time delivery over WebSocket. The UI is intentionally minimal, surfacing only what matters. Key modules:

  • ingest Solana RPC/WebSocket, Jupiter API, and DEX endpoints
  • normalize unify quotes, pairs, and liquidity metadata
  • score probability-weighted profit with TTL and path constraints
  • emit lightweight events to front-end subscribers
WHY SPREADNET

Solana’s throughput and low fees enable fast, granular execution. SpreadNet exploits this by tracking cross-venue inefficiencies, surfacing only actionable context. The goal is not pretty charts but precise signals and robust automation.

TRADING STRATEGIES

SpreadBot employs multiple sophisticated strategies, each optimized for different market conditions:

[●] Cross-DEX Arbitrage Classic price discrepancy exploitation

The foundational strategy that identifies price differences between DEXs for the same trading pair. SpreadBot monitors over 15 major DEXs simultaneously, executing trades when profit margins exceed risk-adjusted thresholds.

[●] Triangular Arbitrage Multi-hop currency conversion inefficiencies

Exploits pricing inconsistencies across currency pairs (e.g., SOL→USDC→RAY→SOL) by executing rapid sequential trades. The AI engine optimizes path selection considering gas costs and slippage across all legs.

[●] Statistical Arbitrage Mean reversion on correlated assets

Advanced strategy using machine learning to identify temporary divergences from historical price relationships. The system builds and maintains correlation matrices for all tracked assets, executing pairs trades when statistical significance thresholds are met.

[●] Liquidity Mining Arbitrage Yield farming optimization with instant rebalancing

Dynamically allocates capital across liquidity pools to maximize yield while maintaining market-neutral exposure. The bot continuously rebalances positions as yields change, capturing both arbitrage profits and farming rewards.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Q: Can I use SpreadBot with less than 15M SPREAD tokens?

A: No, the 15M token minimum is a hard requirement for beta access. This ensures proper incentive alignment and helps us manage capacity during the initial rollout. Future versions may offer tiered access with different capital limits.

Q: How does SpreadBot differ from manual arbitrage trading?

A: SpreadBot operates 24/7 with sub-50ms execution speeds, far faster than any human trader. It also employs sophisticated risk management, portfolio optimization, and MEV protection strategies that would be impossible to implement manually.

Q: What happens if SpreadBot loses money?

A: SpreadBot includes comprehensive risk controls including daily loss limits, position sizing algorithms, and circuit breakers. However, all trading involves risk, and users should be prepared for potential losses. The system will automatically halt trading if daily losses exceed 5%.

Q: Can I withdraw my tokens after gaining SpreadBot access?

A: There's a 90-day minimum commitment period after beta launch. After this period, you can withdraw tokens, but SpreadBot access will be revoked if your balance falls below 15M tokens.

Q: How are profits distributed?

A: Profits are automatically credited to your connected wallet after each successful arbitrage trade. You receive 80% of net profits, while 20% goes to the protocol (used for development, infrastructure, and token burns).
